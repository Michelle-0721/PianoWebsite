Music is a way to bring humans together in a close-knit community. Music is also

a healer to soothe broken hearts and release stress. As a result, her long-term goal

is to integrate music with technology and develop applications that people with or

without a music background can play, enjoy, and even make quality music together

without the constraints of time and location.

Chian-Ru Chen is not a conservatory-trained pianist; she began private piano

lessons at age 3.5. She has learned basic music theory and used it to improvise,

create, and accompany. Eventually, she culminated in her piano composition and

performed in the Junior Original Concert. Besides, she received her advanced

certificate in Piano Grade 5 administered by the Yamaha Grade Examination

System. Equipped with solid music skills, she aspired to impart them to others,

thus organizing a piano trio team and being the accompanist of the class chorus competition. She won an outstanding award in the Student Music Competition

in Hsinchu for the 2012 academic year. Group music experiences have built her confidence, teamwork, and leadership abilities, making her an outstanding

communicator and collaborator.

Chian-Ru Chen is pursuing her master’s degree at the Institute of Electronics and

Engineering at National Tsing Hua University (NTHU) and holds a Bachelor of

Science degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (NTHU). In her

private music training, she is currently instructed by Prof. Hsing-Chwen Hsin. She

has attended master classes with several musicians, such as Karoly Mocsari,

Aquiles Delle Vigne, Mo-Li Chiang, and Huei-Wen Chen.
